Things I Learned This Week

March 20, 2026

A grab bag of things that caught my attention this week.

First, I discovered that Hugo’s summary handling is smarter than I expected. You can either let it auto-truncate or drop a `

` divider wherever you want the break. Small thing, but it changes how you think about structuring a post.

Second, I spent some time reading about color theory for interfaces. Turns out a near-monochrome palette with a single accent color is one of the hardest things to get right, but also one of the most timeless.

Third, coffee tastes better when you grind the beans right before brewing. I know everyone says this. They’re right.
